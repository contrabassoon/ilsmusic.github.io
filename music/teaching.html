Philosphy of Teaching

As a teacher, my philosophy of teaching centers around creating a supportive and nurturing learning environment that empowers students to reach their full potential. My goal is not only to teach students how to play the bassoon, but to also help them develop critical thinking skills, self-discipline, and a lifelong love of music.

To do this, I tailor my teaching to the needs of each student with a "come as you are" approach. This involves assessing each student's current skill level, setting personalized goals, and creating a customized lesson plan that helps them achieve those goals. I also believe in regularly providing constructive feedback, celebrating successes, and encouraging students to learn from their mistakes.

In addition, I believe that music education should not only focus on technical proficiency, but also on musical expression and creativity. Therefore, I encourage students to explore their own musical interests and to experiment with different styles and genres of music. I also believe that music education can play a vital role in building community and fostering cultural understanding, and I strive to expose my students to diverse musical traditions and perspectives.

Finally, I believe that a successful learning experience involves building strong relationships with my students based on mutual respect, trust, and open communication. By establishing a supportive and collaborative learning environment, I hope to inspire my students to not only become better bassoonists, but also to become lifelong learners and passionate music advocates.
